Doppl — Research Overview

Doppl is an experimental project from Google Labs focused on exploring how generative AI can support virtual try‑on experiences. It allows users to upload a full‑body photo and see themselves wearing different outfits through AI‑generated images and short animations. The project is positioned as a research experiment rather than a commercial product, and it is currently available only in select regions.
What Doppl Does

Doppl combines several capabilities into a single experience:
Virtual Try‑On

Users can upload a full‑body photo and apply outfits to themselves using generative AI. Doppl produces:

    A static image of the user wearing the outfit

    A short animated clip showing the outfit in motion

This is designed to help users visualize how different clothing styles might look on them.
Discovery Feed

Doppl includes a personalized feed of AI‑generated outfit videos. The feed adapts based on:

    User preferences

    Items the user interacts with

    Styles the user chooses during onboarding

Try‑On From Any Image

Users can try on outfits from:

    Photos

    Screenshots

    Uploaded product images

This makes Doppl flexible for exploring different styles.
Shopping Integration

Doppl links outfits to purchasable products, allowing users to explore items that match the styles they try on.
How Doppl Works (High‑Level)

Doppl uses generative AI to combine a user’s photo with an outfit image. At a high level, the system:

    Analyzes the user’s pose and body structure

    Segments the clothing item from the uploaded image

    Maps the clothing onto the user’s body

    Adjusts lighting, texture, and folds

    Generates a static or animated output

The animation feature suggests that Doppl uses motion‑aware generative models to create short, consistent movement loops.
Supported Clothing Types

Doppl currently supports:

    Tops

    Bottoms

    Dresses

Some categories are not supported due to complexity or sensitivity, such as:

    Shoes

    Swimwear

    Lingerie

    Accessories

    Sheer or transparent clothing

Limitations

Doppl is experimental, and Google notes several limitations:

    It does not represent actual fit or sizing

    It may generate inaccuracies in body shape or clothing details

    It may fill in missing areas of the body using generative inference

These limitations are typical of early-stage generative try‑on systems.
Privacy Notes

According to the information provided publicly:

    Data is encrypted in transit

    The developer states that data is not shared with third parties

    The app is intended for users 18+

As with any AI system involving personal images, users should review the privacy policy before use.
Why Doppl Matters

Doppl represents Google’s exploration into:

    AI‑assisted shopping

    Personalized fashion recommendations

    Generative avatar creation

    Motion‑aware generative models

    AI‑driven commerce experiences

It also reflects broader trends in the industry, where companies are experimenting with AI to help users visualize products before purchasing.
Future Possibilities

While Doppl is experimental, it hints at potential future directions:

    More advanced virtual try‑on systems

    AI‑powered personal stylists

    Integration with shopping platforms

    Avatar‑based personalization across apps

    Improved garment simulation and motion modeling

These areas are active research topics in the AI and retail technology space.
